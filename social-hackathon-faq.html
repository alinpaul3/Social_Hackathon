FAQs.

What is The Social Hackathon?
The Social Hackathon (TSH) brings together tech innovators and social entrepreneurs to create solutions that drive positive change and address critical societal challenges. It's a collaborative event where participants form teams to develop prototype solutions to real-world problems within a limited timeframe.
When and where will TSH 2025 take place?
TSH 2025 will be held from March 10-12, 2025. The event will take place at the Global Innovation Center. Remote participation options are also available for international participants.
Who can participate?
TSH is open to developers, designers, entrepreneurs, subject matter experts, students, and anyone passionate about using technology to solve social problems. Teams can consist of 3-5 members with diverse skill sets. We encourage participation from underrepresented groups in tech.
What are this year's challenge tracks?
The 2025 tracks include: Climate Action & Sustainability, Digital Inclusion & Accessibility, Mental Health & Wellbeing, and Ethical AI for Social Good. Teams can choose to work on any of these areas or propose their own challenge within the broader theme of social innovation.
Is there an entry fee?
No, participation in TSH 2025 is completely free. We believe in removing barriers to participation in order to maximize diversity and inclusion.
What resources will be provided?
Participants will have access to: high-speed internet, workspace, mentorship from industry experts, workshops on relevant technologies, meals and refreshments, and API access from our sponsors. Teams are expected to bring their own devices.
What are the prizes?
The winning team will receive $15,000 in seed funding, incubation support, and the opportunity to present at the Global Social Innovation Summit. Track winners will receive $5,000 each. Additionally, all participants will be eligible for recruitment opportunities with our partner organizations.
How will projects be judged?
Projects will be evaluated based on: social impact potential, technical innovation, feasibility and sustainability, user experience design, and presentation quality. Our panel of judges includes social entrepreneurs, technical experts, and impact investors.
Do I need to have a team before registering?
No, you can register individually and form teams at our pre-event networking session or during the first day of the hackathon. We have a team formation platform to help you connect with potential teammates based on complementary skills.
What should I bring?
Participants should bring their laptops, chargers, any specialized hardware needed for their projects, and a government-issued ID for check-in. Comfortable clothing and a reusable water bottle are also recommended.
Is there a code of conduct?
Yes, all participants must adhere to our Code of Conduct which promotes a respectful, inclusive, and collaborative environment. This includes guidelines on appropriate behavior, communication, and project content. The full Code of Conduct is available on our website.
What happens to the intellectual property created?
Teams retain full ownership of their projects. However, we encourage open-source approaches and sharing of learnings. If you accept certain sponsor prizes, there may be specific IP terms attached, which will be clearly communicated.
